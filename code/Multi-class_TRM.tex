\section{General State Variables and Effective Occupancy Limits}
Let the highway be partitioned into $X$ sequential cells indexed by $x\in\{1,2,...,X\}$, each with a physical length $\Delta x$. We introduce a discrete lane index $l\in\{1,2,...,L\}$. Let the index $m$ represent a specific vehicle class ranging from 1 to $M$, and $i$ represent a specific speed category from 1 to $N$, associated with actual physical speed scalar $v_{i}$ $(v_{1}<v_{2}<\dots<v_{N})$.

The universal 3D state variable is $f_{i,x,l}^{(m)}$, representing the absolute density of vehicle class $m$ traveling at speed $v_{i}$ within spatial cell $x$ and lane $l$.

To rigorously capture the heterogeneous spatial footprints of different vehicle typologies, we assign a dimensionless Passenger Car Equivalent (PCE) spatial weight $w^{(m)}$ to each class $m$. The universal physical capacity limit $\rho_{\max}$ is strictly governed by the \textbf{Effective Spatial Occupancy Density}:
\begin{equation}
\Omega_{x,l} = \sum_{m=1}^{M}\sum_{i=1}^{N} w^{(m)} f_{i,x,l}^{(m)}
\end{equation}
All subsequent congestion evaluations, dynamic transition rates, and asymptotic limits must strictly operate against this effective occupancy $\Omega_{x,l}$ to preserve multidimensional physical consistency and prevent unphysical spatial folding.
